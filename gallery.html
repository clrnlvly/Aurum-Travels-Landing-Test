"Calendar of Dreams: Plan Your Perfect Trip by Month"

Select a Month


January: Philippines


Why: The dry season is perfect for island hopping and exploring natural wonders.

Must-Visit: Palawan’s El Nido and Coron for stunning lagoons, Cebu for the Sinulog Festival, and Baguio for cooler weather.

Unique Activity: Dive into the world-famous Tubbataha Reef, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, if diving season starts early.


February: Vietnam


Why: February offers mild weather and cultural richness during Tet, the Vietnamese Lunar New Year.

Must-Visit: Hanoi’s Old Quarter, Halong Bay for cruises, and Hoi An for its lantern-lit streets during Tet.

Unique Activity: Trek the Sapa rice terraces or take a scenic motorbike ride along the Hai Van Pass.


March: Thailand


Why: Enjoy the warm, dry season for beaches and northern adventures.

Must-Visit: Chiang Mai for culture, Phuket for beaches, and Ayutthaya for history.

Unique Activity: Join a Thai cooking class or visit an elephant sanctuary for ethical wildlife experiences.


April: Cambodia


Why: Celebrate Khmer New Year (Choul Chnam Thmey) in a vibrant atmosphere.

Must-Visit: Angkor Wat at sunrise, Phnom Penh’s Royal Palace, and the floating villages of Tonle Sap Lake.

Unique Activity: Participate in traditional Khmer games and water fights during the New Year celebrations.


May: Malaysia


Why: Great weather for outdoor activities and exploring Malaysia’s diversity.

Must-Visit: Kota Kinabalu for trekking Mount Kinabalu, Penang for street food, and Langkawi for pristine beaches.

Unique Activity: Go for a canopy walk in the Danum Valley rainforest or spot turtles nesting in Terengganu.


June: Indonesia


Why: The dry season is perfect for both beach relaxation and jungle trekking.

Must-Visit: Bali’s Ubud for culture, Nusa Penida for dramatic cliffs, and Komodo National Park to see the dragons.

Unique Activity: Witness the Mount Bromo sunrise or dive with manta rays in Nusa Lembongan.


July: Timor-Leste


Why: The weather is dry, making it perfect for outdoor adventures.

Must-Visit: Dili for its cultural sites, Atauro Island for diving, and Jaco Island for secluded beaches.

Unique Activity: Experience the pristine underwater world with some of the best coral reefs in the world.


August: Singapore


Why: The city’s vibrant energy is on full display during National Day (August 9th).

Must-Visit: Marina Bay Sands, Gardens by the Bay, and Chinatown.

Unique Activity: Watch the spectacular National Day Parade and fireworks or try the Singapore Food Festival specials.


September: Laos


Why: Lush green landscapes after the rainy season make it perfect for nature lovers.

Must-Visit: Luang Prabang for its temples, the Plain of Jars for history, and Vang Vieng for adventure.

Unique Activity: Cruise down the Mekong River or explore the hidden caves and waterfalls in the countryside.


October: Brunei


Why: The dry weather is ideal for exploring cultural and natural landmarks.

Must-Visit: The stunning Omar Ali Saifuddien Mosque , Kampong Ayer water village, and Ulu Temburong National Park.

Unique Activity: Take a canopy walk in the rainforest or visit the Royal Regalia Museum to learn about Brunei’s heritage.


November: Myanmar


Why: The cool and dry weather makes this month perfect for exploring the country’s iconic sites.

Must-Visit: Bagan for hot air balloon rides, Mandalay for its palaces, and Inle Lake for its stilt villages.

Unique Activity: Witness the famous leg-rowing fishermen on Inle Lake or visit the sacred Shwedagon Pagoda in Yangon.


December: Philippines


Why: The festive season is a cultural highlight, with Christmas being deeply celebrated across the country.

Must-Visit: Manila for colorful Christmas lights, Vigan for heritage charm, and Boracay for holiday beach vibes.

Unique Activity: Attend a Simbang Gabi (night mass) or enjoy local delicacies at festive markets.
